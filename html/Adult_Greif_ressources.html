Handling the Holdays

"Adults often think of children as being 'resilient' or 'too young to understand death and greif.' The truth is...all children are vulnerble to feelings of sadness,depression,anger,fear and anxiety when someone they love dies. Any child old enough to laugh and experience pleasure is also old enough to expericence the pain of greif." So Robyn Plocher writes to parents and caregivers of breaved children.

The holiday season, regardless of what treaditions your family practices, can be a difficualt time when someone you love has died. As you struggle to decide what you will do and who you will be with when the holiday arrives it is quite likely you haven't given much thought to the children in the family.

Children pick on our strss and disress. Children have their own worries about how the hoidays will be diffrent. Children grieve the absence of their family member, too.

This woorkbook is designed to help children to express, through words and pictures, what family treaditions are most important to them and what worries they have. The workbook is designed to facilitate communication between the child and his/her parent or caregiver. Sit with your child while they compleate the workbook or make sure they share it with you after it is competed. The last page is a list of family activites and projects you can work on together. Use these pages to begin a dialogue and to inform your decisions about holiday observances.
